# Lab 2 · Log a few transactions

## What you'll do
Duration: 1:00

MilesVault is most fun once it has your real points and miles in it. This lab gets you there the **easy way** — everything here is clicking and chatting, **no beancount at all** (that comes later, only if you want it).

In a few minutes you'll:

- Add a card and a loyalty programme
- Give a programme its current balance
- Log a purchase and an award redemption **just by typing in plain English**

By the end your vault has real data — and the next lab turns that into the good stuff: award searches, transfer paths, status.

> aside positive
> 
> Everything below uses **made-up example values** — follow along with these, then do it for real with your own cards afterwards.

## Add a card and a programme
Duration: 3:00

1. On [**Home**](https://milesvault.com/vault), click **+ Card**.
2. Search for a card you hold — e.g. *HDFC Infinia* — and **Save**.
3. Click **+ Programme**, search for one — e.g. *KrisFlyer* — and **Save**.

You'll see them appear on your vault under **Credit cards** and **Programmes**, balances empty for now.

> aside positive
> 
> This is the same as the **Add accounts** chip inside the Journal chat — use whichever you find first.

## Give your programme a balance
Duration: 2:00

Your KrisFlyer tile shows zero — let's tell it what you actually have.

1. Open the [**Journal**](https://milesvault.com/editor) and click the **Update balance** chip (the scales icon).
2. Pick **Assets:Rewards:KrisFlyer**, enter **20000**, currency **KRISFLYER**, and today's date.
3. Confirm.

Head back to **Home** — KrisFlyer now shows **20,000 miles**. (Behind the scenes it wrote a tidy ledger entry; you'll learn exactly what in the "Under the hood" labs.)

## Log a purchase — just type it
Duration: 3:00

1. In the [**Journal**](https://milesvault.com/editor) chat, type in plain words:

> *Spent 4,000 on a flight booking on my HDFC Infinia*

2. The assistant drafts a **Proposed transaction** — the spend *and* the reward points the card earned.
3. Glance at it and click **Approve**.

That's it — your card's outstanding balance and its reward points both update on **Home**.

> aside positive
> 
> You don't tell it the earn rate — MilesVault knows your card's rules and fills the points in. You just confirm.

## Log an award redemption
Duration: 2:00

Let's spend some of those miles. In the chat, type:

> *Booked a Singapore Airlines award flight for 7,125 KrisFlyer miles, worth about ₹15,000*

Approve the draft. Your KrisFlyer balance drops to **12,875**, and the trip shows up in your travel spending at its cash value.

> aside positive
> 
> Notice how natural this is — describe what happened, review, approve. That's 90% of using MilesVault day to day.

## Bring in a whole month (optional)
Duration: 1:00

For real life, you won't type every line. Drop in a statement and MilesVault drafts the lot:

1. Open [**Statements**](https://milesvault.com/statements) → **Upload statement** → drop a PDF.
2. It drafts in the background; review and approve from the Statements page.

Or set a **forwarding rule** so your bank's transaction emails draft themselves (see [**Inbox**](https://milesvault.com/inbox)).
