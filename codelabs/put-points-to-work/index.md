# Lab 3 · Put your points to work

## Why this is the whole point
Duration: 1:00

You've got real balances in your vault now. This is where MilesVault earns its name: it turns "I have some miles somewhere" into "here's exactly where they can take me, and how to get more."

In this lab:

- **Award Explorer** — where your miles can fly you, and whether you can afford it
- **Paths to Points** — how to reach any programme from your cards
- **Status Match** — turn one status into another
- Reading your **vault** and your **spending**

## Award Explorer — where can these miles go?
Duration: 3:00

1. Open [**Award Explorer**](https://milesvault.com/explore) from the menu.
2. Type an origin and destination — e.g. **BLR → NRT** — and pick a cabin (**Economy / Premium / Business / First**).
3. You'll see every programme that flies that route, split into **Direct** and **Connecting**, each with its **points cost** and operating airlines.

Because you hold KrisFlyer miles, affordable awards get a green **"You have the points"** chip. For the rest, expand a row to see a **transfer path** — how your other points could get you there.

> aside positive
> 
> Use the **Filters** to set a transfer source, limit stops, or include/exclude airlines. Sort by **Cheapest**, **Fewest stops**, or **Shortest**.

## Paths to Points — how do I reach a programme?
Duration: 3:00

Found an award in a programme you don't hold? See how to get there.

1. Open [**Points**](https://milesvault.com/points).
2. Choose a target — e.g. **Qantas** or **Avios**.
3. You get an interactive map of every **card and currency** that can reach it within a few transfer hops — each edge showing the **ratio** (e.g. ×1.5), and a node showing what it'd cost to simply **buy** the points.

Toggle **My points** to see only what *you* hold, or **All points** for the full universe.

> aside positive
> 
> Cards you actually hold are ringed in green — so the routes you can use *right now* jump out.

## Status Match — trade one status for another
Duration: 2:00

1. Open [**Status Match**](https://milesvault.com/status-match).
2. Pick a status you hold (e.g. *United Gold*) and, optionally, one you want.
3. You'll see a map of every match path — **solid = free**, **dashed = paid** — colour-coded by alliance, with a count like *"4 matches available"*.

If you've recorded status tiers in your vault, they're pre-filled and tagged **"you hold"**.

## Read your vault
Duration: 2:00

Back on [**Home**](https://milesvault.com/vault), everything's at a glance — points and miles first:

- **Programmes** — miles/points balances, what's still **pending**, progress toward elite status
- **Credit cards** — what each card is earning you, plus its balance and monthly spend
- **Outstanding** and **Spent · this month** — the money context behind it all

Click any tile to drill into its history.

## See where your money goes
Duration: 2:00

1. Open [**Expenses**](https://milesvault.com/accounts).
2. You get a drillable treemap of your spending — switch between **Expenses / Income / Assets / Liabilities** and time ranges (**1M / 3M / 6M / YTD / All**).
3. Click a tile to drill in; at the leaf level you see the actual transactions, with a link to open them in the Journal.

This is how you spot **which card earns most where**, so your next swipe is the smart one.
