The fastest way to bring in a month of activity.

  1. Open Statements in the left menu.
  2. Click Upload statement.
  3. Drop in your PDF, or click Browse....

MilesVault reads it and drafts everything in the background — you don't wait. When it's ready, you review and approve the drafts from the Statements page.

You can also drag a PDF anywhere in the app to start an upload.

If the PDF is password-protected, you'll be asked for the password. It's unlocked in your browser and never stored — only the text inside is sent for drafting.

The most hands-off option: let your bank's transaction-alert emails update your vault automatically.

  1. Open Inbox in the left menu.
  2. Copy your personal address, shown as "Forward transaction emails to ingest+...@milesvault.com".
  3. In Gmail (or your mail app), create a forwarding rule that sends those bank alerts to that address.

From then on, new transactions draft themselves in your Inbox — you just review and approve.

Keep this address private — anyone who has it could send things into your inbox. If it ever leaks, hit Rotate on the Inbox page to get a fresh one (and update your forwarding rule).

For the odd purchase that isn't on a statement yet — a cash-style entry, a quick correction — just use the Journal chat, as you did in earlier labs:

Paid 1,200 for petrol on my Axis Magnus

The assistant drafts it; you approve. Between statements, forwarding, and chat, you'll rarely type raw beancount again — though you always can.

Three inputs, one ledger: statements for bulk, forwarding for ongoing, chat for one-offs. Pick whatever's handy in the moment.

This is where your training matters. Every draft — from a statement, an email, or the chat — is a proposal you approve, never an automatic write. Before you tap Approve, give it the eye you've trained:

  • Does the expense category make sense, or did a merchant get miscategorised?
  • Are the reward points there, in the right pending bucket?
  • Does it balance (it won't let you save otherwise — but check the figures match the statement)?
  • For forex or cashback, is the fee or credit split out correctly?

If something's off, fix it inline before approving, or reject it and nudge the assistant.
