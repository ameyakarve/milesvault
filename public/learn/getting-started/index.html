MilesVault is an operating system for your points and miles — the one place where every reward your cards earn lives, and where you actually put it to work. It tracks what you hold and what's still pending across all your cards and programmes, then helps you spend it well: award flights, point transfers, elite status.

This is a short series of hands-on labs. This first one gets you signed in and oriented — the later labs teach you the ledger that runs it all, one small step at a time.

In this lab you'll:

  • Join the Discord and sign in
  • Meet your vault
  • Learn your way around — and where the community lives

No technical background needed here. It's all clicking and looking around. The typing comes later — and we walk you through every line.

The welcome email that brought you here has your Discord invite — that's where access, updates, and the community all live.

  1. Open that email and tap the Discord invite to join the MilesVault server.
  2. Head to MilesVault and sign in:

Open MilesVault

  1. Click Continue with Discord and approve the prompt. You'll land on Home — your vault.

That's the only sign-in there is — no separate password to remember. Your Discord account is your key, so make sure you've joined the server first.

If sign-in bounces you back to the start, your Discord account just hasn't been cleared yet. Say hello in the server and you'll be sorted in no time.

The first time you sign in, your vault is empty. You'll see "Your vault is empty — tell the Assistant about a card you hold, or drop a statement," with an Open the Journal button. That's fine — you'll fill it up in the next labs.

Once you've added cards and logged a few things, your Home comes alive — points and miles first:

  • Programmes — your airline and hotel balances as Miles or Points, what's still pending, and your progress toward elite status.
  • Credit cards — what each card is earning you (with "N pending" for points not yet credited), alongside its balance and monthly spend.
  • Outstanding and Spent · this month — the spending context behind it all: what you owe, and where the money (and therefore the earning) went.

Empty now is exactly right. Lab 2 onward is where you start putting things in — by hand and with the AI.

Down the left side is the menu you'll use to get around. (On a phone it's behind the ☰ button, top-left.) Each item is a place you can open right now:

  • Home — your vault.
  • Journal — where you read and write your ledger, and chat with the AI. You'll live here in the next labs.
  • Statements — upload a PDF statement.
  • Inbox — transaction emails you forward in land here.
  • Expenses — a drill-down of where your money goes.

And the three planning tools — the reason MilesVault exists:

  • Award Explorer — pick where you want to fly and a cabin, and see which programmes get you there, what it costs in points, and whether you can afford it now or with a transfer.
  • Points — choose a programme you want and see every route from your cards to reach it, and at what ratio.
  • Status Match — see how to turn one airline or hotel status into another, free or paid.

You don't need all of these today. Each lab introduces the ones it uses, so you're never staring at a screen wondering what it's for.

You're not on your own:

  • A floating feedback button sits in the corner of every screen — tap it to flag anything confusing or broken (you can even attach a screenshot). We read every one.
  • Hang out in #general on the Discord — ask questions, share finds, or just chat points and miles with everyone else.
  • Drop into the daily voice channel at 9pm IST — to ask anything live, hear what's new, and talk strategy.

MilesVault is in beta. Rough edges are expected — your feedback and questions are exactly how it gets better, so don't be shy.
